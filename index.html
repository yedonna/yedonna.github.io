hello

welcome to a simple introduction -

for those both new and old to milk tea.

here in norcal, we bay area residents love our milk tea.

milk tea is more than just adding a splash of milk to tea. there are many milk tea recipes found around the world, particularly in Asia, each with their own distinct flavors. in this guide, we will be focusing on three classic recipes: taiwanese boba/bubble milk tea, hong kong milk tea, and masala chai.

recipes

taiwanese milk tea
Ingredients
  • 3 parts water
  • 2 parts white sugar
  • 1 part brown sugar
  • 4 parts water (or more)
  • 1 part tapioca pearls
  • 3 oz tapioca pearls
  • 1 cup brewed tea (cooled)
  • 1 cup milk
  • 4 ice cubes
Steps
  1. Prep syrup. Bring water to boil, add sugars, simmmer until dissolved.
  2. Prep tapioca pearls. Follow instructions on packaging, place in syrup.
  3. Add the tapioca pearls to cup. In a cocktail shaker, combine the tea, milk, and ice cubes. Pour and enjoy.
hong kong milk tea
Ingredients
  • 1 cup of water
  • 2 tbsp. black tea leaves (preferably a bold Ceylon tea)
  • 1 can of evaporated milk
  • 1 can of sweet condensed milk
  • (optional) sugar
Steps
  1. Combine water and tea leaves in a small saucespan over medium heat.
  2. Bring to a low boil. Reduce heat and simmer for 3-5 min.
  3. Remove from heat. Strain tea.
  4. Pour tea and evaporated milk to cup, 7:3 ratio.
  5. Add sweet condensed milk to taste. (Optional) Add sugar to taste. Enjoy.
masala chai
Ingredients
  • 2 cups milk
  • 2 cups water
  • 4 whole cloves
  • 2 cardamom pods (crushed)
  • 2 perppercorns (crushed)
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1/2-inch piece ginger (grated)
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 2 tbsp black tea (preferably Assam tea)
Steps
  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the milk, water, and spices. Simmer over medium heat for 10 min, stirring occasionally.
  2. Add the sugar and tea leaves. Stir, and then simmer for 5 minutes.
  3. Strain into glasses or mugs and serve. Enjoy.
